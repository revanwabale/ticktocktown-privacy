Tick-Tock Town — Privacy Policy

No data collected

Last updated: 21 June 2026

Tick-Tock Town is a clock-learning game for young children (ages 4–7). We built it to be completely private and safe. The app does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal information whatsoever.

What we collect

Nothing. Tick-Tock Town has:

  • No accounts, sign-in, or registration.
  • No analytics, tracking, or advertising of any kind.
  • No third-party SDKs or services.
  • No access to the camera, microphone, photos, contacts, or location.
  • No network connection — the app works fully offline.

What stays on the device

Your child's game progress (stars, stickers, and which lessons they've seen) and the sound/voice settings are saved only on the iPad itself, using Apple's standard on-device storage. This information never leaves the device, is never sent to us or anyone else, and is removed if the app is deleted. A grown-up can erase it any time from the in-app “Grown-Ups” area (Reset all progress).

Children's privacy

Tick-Tock Town is designed for children and is compliant with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and Apple's Kids Category requirements: because we collect no data at all, there is nothing to disclose, request consent for, or delete. Grown-up controls are placed behind a press-and-hold parental gate so they aren't reached by accident.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new date above.
